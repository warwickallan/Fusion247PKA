## The model (substantive reconstruction)

**Who / what.** Clients = small business owners, ~2–20 employees, ~$500k–5M revenue. Offer = a **45-min structured interview → prescribe 3–7 off-the-shelf tools → reclaim ~7 hrs/week (avg)**, with a **money-back guarantee** if <5 hrs of opportunity is found. Positioning: "we prescribe, we don't build" — accessible even to non-technical operators. Rationale: ~95% of SMEs use no AI beyond ChatGPT prompting — a "blue ocean."

**Four phases**
1. **Discovery call** (Zoom/Meet + an AI note-taker — Fathom/Otter/Fireflies). *Probing only, no pitching.* Questions: "walk me through yesterday", "what tasks do you dread", "where does work pile up", "what have you failed to automate", and the killer: *"if you could wave a magic wand and delete one process, what would it be?"* (usual answer: email). Discipline: **bite your tongue — don't prescribe on the call.**
2. **AI analysis** of the transcript. Feed it to **Claude** (they built a **Claude Skill**) to surface pain points + research off-the-shelf tools that fix them. **QA step:** don't take Claude's output at face value — substitute inappropriate picks (e.g. not Salesforce for a 4-person landscaper). **Evals/context:** feed past transcripts + finished reports back into the skill so "Claude knows what good looks like" — first 2–3 are ~60–70% right; by the 4th–6th it's often copy-paste. Tool directories used: **futurepedia.io** (acquired by HubSpot), **theresanaiforthat.com** (filter by industry).
3. **The report** — the client-facing deliverable (built in Claude's design tool; free template at **audittemplate.ai**, plug-and-play). Sections: **executive summary** (1–2 pain points, outcome, hours reclaimed, "primary focus"); **effort-vs-impact matrix** (top-left = *quick wins*: high-impact/low-effort = the off-the-shelf tools; top-right = *major projects* = the upsell hooks); **recommended solutions** (tool, pain point, cost, setup time, time saved); a **4-day quick-start plan** (≤10 min/day so a "confused mind" still acts); and a **financial-impact** slide (net monthly ROI = weekly hours reclaimed × hourly rate − tool cost; avg tools ~$60/mo → **always 4-figure ROI**). Mantra: *"a confused mind doesn't buy, implement, or upsell"* → make it stupid-simple. "Primary focus" = one of **three ROI levers: effectiveness (more money) / efficiency (save time) / quality**.
4. **Review call** (~30 min, screen-share the report). Three closing questions: "which is most urgent?", "do it yourself or want my help?", "what's your timeline?" → **50–60% ask for implementation.**

**The upsell ladder** (the $999 is "just the door"): (1) **process redesign** — fix a broken process before automating it (map current → future state; ~$3–3.5k); (2) **automation build** — Zapier/make/n8n for cut-and-dry 1–3 step flows (~$1.5k); (3) **knowledge system** — e.g. a custom GPT trained on a business broker's listing so buyers self-serve the FAQ (pulls efficiency + quality levers); (4) **custom workflows** — Claude Skills + reference files for proprietary processes, with a **recurring maintenance retainer** as processes change; plus a favourite recurring-revenue "AI concierge" offer. **Self-liquidating funnel:** put ~$200 of each $999 into Meta ads → get *paid* to acquire customers.
