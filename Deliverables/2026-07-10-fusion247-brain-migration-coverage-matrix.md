## 1. Overall coverage

**Roughly three-quarters of the inventoried Fusion247 Brain capabilities (32 of 43 rows) are `absorbed` or `mapped-to-existing`** in myPKA — but that headline number is doing more work than it looks like, and the composition matters more than the ratio:

- **14 rows are `absorbed`** — concentrated in two clusters: (a) the Client-Delivery/Warden buildout this session and the parent task actually did (rows 1, 3, 4, 5, 6), and (b) pre-existing myPKA scaffold machinery that happens to already satisfy nearly every item on Fusion's own `F247.proposal.mypka-gap-analysis` (rows 20, 25–31) — machinery that predates this reconciliation effort entirely, not something built *because of* it.
- **18 rows are `mapped-to-existing`**, several explicitly flagged as **partial/narrower** matches (rows 7, 12, 15, 22, 32, 35) — a different mechanism solving a related but not identical problem. Treat these as directionally covered, not verified equivalent in full.
- **1 row is `retained-as-source`** (the live BRK-NPL-001 project data, deliberately out of scope).
- **1 row is `rejected`** (Write Methods — not applicable to myPKA's runtime, my own judgment, not a user decision).
- **5 rows are `deferred`** — entire named personal-domain systems (CareerAIR, AsdAIr, TubeAIR, ICOR notes, Anti-AI Writing Pack) with zero myPKA presence.
- **4 rows are `unresolved`** — most importantly CategorisAIr's general-purpose source-triage engine (row 13), which is the single largest structural gap in this audit.

**The honest framing for the user:** the Client-Delivery/Warden slice this migration effort actually focused on is well covered and well cited. The *rest* of Fusion247 Brain — the personal-domain agents (CareerAIR, AsdAIr, TubeAIR) and the general-purpose source-processing engine (CategorisAIr) — has had essentially no attention. The high absorbed/mapped percentage is inflated by the fact that myPKA's pre-existing scaffold happened to independently solve most of Fusion's own governance gap-analysis (P1–P12, S1–S4) — that's a genuinely good finding, but it is not evidence that Fusion247's *content* (the personal-domain knowledge and agents) has been migrated. Those are two different questions and this matrix answers both separately.
