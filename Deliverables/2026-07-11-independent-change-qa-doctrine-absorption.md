## Source-hygiene caveat — Critical Panel Review duplicate Drive copies (added 2026-07-11, round 3)

A direct Google Drive check (performed by Larry, not independently re-verified by Pax this pass — no Drive connector available in this session's toolset) found **three** files titled or near-identically titled "F247.skill.critical-panel-review" in the relevant Drive folder:

- One explicitly self-labelled `F247.skill.critical-panel-review - superseded duplicate-write error`, created `2026-07-10T03:15:32Z`.
- Two others sharing the exact identical title `F247.skill.critical-panel-review`, with no distinguishing marker, created `2026-07-10T03:13:45Z` and `2026-07-10T03:21:41Z` respectively — roughly 8 minutes apart.

`critical-panel-fresh.txt` — the file actually read for row #27 and used throughout this matrix — cannot be attributed to one specific one of these three copies with confidence. On direct inspection, the scratchpad capture itself contains the full skill text repeated three times back-to-back, byte-for-byte identical across all three internal copies, with no "superseded" label, title variant, or date marker distinguishing any of the three internal repeats from one another. This is consistent with (though it does not prove) more than one of the three Drive copies having been fetched and concatenated into this one scratchpad file at some point, rather than a single clean capture of one specific, identified copy. **Stated honestly: it is unclear which of the two unmarked duplicates (created `03:13:45Z` or `03:21:41Z`) this scratchpad file was actually read from, or whether it in fact represents more than one of the three Drive copies pulled together.**

This does not change the decision already made at row #27 — Critical Panel Review stays a separate, callable skill, never inlined — but it is a real source-hygiene gap in its own right: nobody should assume every Drive copy titled "F247.skill.critical-panel-review" is a separately accepted version, and any future reviewer relying on this matrix's characterization of `critical-panel-fresh.txt` should treat its specific-copy provenance as unconfirmed, not settled.
