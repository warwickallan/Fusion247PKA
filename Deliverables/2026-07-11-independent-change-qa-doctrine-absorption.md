## Headline dispositions (summary)

- **already-covered (4 rows: #2, #14, #15 blanket instance, #21 in spirit):** VerifiAIr/skill boundary, fabricated-reference detection, blanket unlogged-change detection, and report-only-by-default all already live correctly in Larry's Duty 2 and SOP-017. SOP-018 cross-links; it does not re-implement.
- **strengthen-existing (7 rows: #7, #9, #11, #13, #17, #22, #23):** Existing mechanisms extended narrowly to cover "as applied to one specific reviewed change" rather than duplicated wholesale.
- **create-in-new-SOP (14 rows):** The genuine substance of SOP-018 — review-window definition, source-of-truth precedence, boundary-drift checking, the five-tier severity scheme, the formal verdict, literal-re-read discipline, structural-damage-after-edits checking, and — most importantly — the same-model-review honesty rule.
- **intentionally-not-absorbed (4 rows: #12, #18, #27, plus the Drive-specific mechanics folded into #13):** Drive-native mechanisms with no myPKA equivalent, or skills/concepts that must stay out-of-scope by explicit instruction.

## The one finding worth flagging above the rest

Row #26. Fusion247 Brain's `/update QA` was never actually "same-model reviewing itself" — it was baked-in cross-model by construction (Claude auditing GPT/Fusion). Fusion247PKA/myPKA's normal operating mode is the opposite: one model, one session, every specialist a hat-switch. A naive port of the old checklist would have quietly smuggled in a false sense of independence. That's the exact gap Larry's mandated "Same-model review — not independently verified" honesty clause exists to close, and it's the one piece of this doctrine that isn't really a port at all — it's a correction the port itself made necessary.
